Last thoughts on America...

I leave for the airport in seven hours. From there I'll be leaving to Chicago for two days of "pre-training" before I head off on an all night flight to Bucharest, and I'm sitting here wondering, what am I gonna miss about the United States. It'd be pompous to say nothing, and after already briefly traveling abroad I know some of the things I'll miss.
First off will be the food. I HATE Mcdonalds, it tastes aweful, but some of the best meals I've had abroad were from this very place. For some reason the greasy Mickey-D's burgers just taste like home. But on top of this is the variety of food I can recieve in the United States. Livign in Tacoma I especially had the feeling of variety of food, and places to eat. Sure, there were sandwhich makers, and thai food places, teriyaki and chinese...mexican too! The thing I've noticed before in traveling is that your options for eating out, are never quite the same. Maybe Romania will be different, and honestally I don't know, but the first thing I know I'll miss will definately be the food I can get here in the US.
After the food, what else is there...well, for me there's the political process and how involved someone can be. In many ways I think I've just increased my involvement with our countries politics, however, I also just recently shot myself in the foot! I can't vote this year, next year, and maybe the year after. Why, oh...it's my own damn fault honestally! When my parents moved recently, I forgot to update my drivers liscense, which means I can't register within St. Paul to vote, and that if I want an accurate ID card, I can't go out and get a new one. I chose having an ID card that's not about to expire; however, this doesn't change the fact that it's still a fake now in all technicality. Well, I'm rambling, but in the end...I CAN'T VOTE NEXT YEAR!!! AHHHHH!!! I shouldn't worry too much, Minnesota hasn't gone republican in 30+ years now, so I'm still safe.
My own mistakes asside the greatest thing I think I'm gonna miss about the United States is going to be the easy access I have to any number of communication sources. I've been told that internet and cell phones are becoming increasingly rampent throughout Romania; however, my ability (even with the use of a cell phone or email) to access friends and family here within the US just seems very limited. This can be due to a number of things, but I think mostly to international calling rates along with about an 8-10 hour time zone difference.
It's weird, in no way have I seen near everything in this country, but at the same time it seems more appealing to take opportunities to go to the ends of the earth to see anything else but America, to go for an adventure into the unknown. I will miss America. I will miss the easy access interstates, better-than-starbucks cafes on ever corner of ever town. I'll miss the local bars, who's tenders know my face. I already miss the cd stores, and the dent my music collection will take these next two years. The book stores, fast food and chains, the ability to go into any target store in America and already know its layout before entering. Driving on the right hand side of the road may definitely become missed (hell, I never got used to it the last time I traveled either!).
In the end, these are the things that are running through my head now six hours prior to departure, six hours away from the unknown! I'm probably being overally dramatic, but hell...I can't come back to any of this for two years...so I think I'm granted a little leeway.
ttfn-ta ta for now
Ken

PS-upon rethinking some of this, I will NOT miss the whole in my wallet that Besy Buy burns me with on an almost biweekly basis it seems like...see yall in a couple years.
