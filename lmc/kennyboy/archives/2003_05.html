KEN'S First Post-Grad Book Report: (Grade F+)
I've been doing a fair amount of reading recently. Furthermore, I think I should mention it's great to NEVER AGAIN have requirered reading; not that I've disliked everything I've read over the past 4 years, reading is just more enjoyable when it's on your own time.
But enough of that little tangent, the current books on my reading list include Robert Kaplan's Balkan Ghosts, which entails much of Kaplan's own adventures traveling through parts of Europe, and especially Romania after the 1989 revoltuion. Overal, this painted a very bleak picture of Romania, and of Romania's past leaders. If I have time I intend to read about his adventures in other countries, and also strongly recomend this for anyone's casual reading.
The Rough Guide to Romania was next on my list. This is a basic travel book, with places to stay. Oh, and why did I chose this one? Well, because they [the Peace Corps] told me to, and that's damn well good enough reason for me.
The next book was recomended to me by Prof. O'Neil and the Peace Corps, and is Petter Hessler's River Town, and to be honest I havne't had a chance to get to this one quite yet.
The final book I'm going through at the moment is...and don't laugh, Dracula by Bram Stoker. Honestally, after reading Kaplan, and how many times he sited this book in Balkan Ghosts, I couldn't resist adding it to my list of books to read before going to Romania. Oh, and if you're interested, I think Dracula is highly entertaining too...good easy reading, which I have not found myself involved in enough lately.

My First Entry

Hi everyong, this is the first entry in what I hope to be many more to come of my experiences teaching secondary school english in Romania. For those of you who stumbled onto this page, it was set up by my good friend Buck in order for me to easily communicate with everyone else about what I'm doing, and this way no one gets any of those annoying mass emails; or at least it will decrease the number of annoying mass emails you'll all recieve.
Anyway, currently I'm at home awaiting to leave for Chicago on June 9th, and then will head to Bucharest a few days latter. I'm in the process of researching the country and the culture of Romania, and have begun with Robert Kaplan's Balkan Ghosts, which is a history of Eastern European countries.
I hope everyone is doing well, and I will add more to this latter when I know that it works.
