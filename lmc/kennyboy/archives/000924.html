My new home

A few days ago I just returned from Southren Bucovina where I had the opportunity to see my new appartment where I'll be staying for the next two years. The appartment itslef is wonderful, well...at least by peace corps standards because there's no lasting smell as if something has just died within the vicinity of the building. In fact, the principle of my new school owns the appartment, so it's staying in pretty good condition. I have the usual kitchen, bathroom, living room and bedroom (and closed in glass balcony). Outside one window is a monastery and some mountains, and outside the other side of the apartment is, well...more apartments, but more mountains and a ski slope beyond it. In the long run, I think I can make due.
The town itself is small and wonderful. It reminds me much of how Montana looks, or at least it reminds me much of how I think Montana would look if it had never been clear cut or had an Interstate going through it (it takes almost two hours to drive 48 miles and 9 hours to get there by train from Bucharest, and all of this is in a country the size of Oregon).
I live above the internet cafe, and one of three stores that sells food in town. The internet cafes and cell phone stores, from which now I am a consumer of both, make me question the priorities of the region I'm in. I say this because I have no hot water...EVER, and no water at all for at least 6-8 hours a day. On top of this I also never really get any heat, and have to start chopping wood in order to heat my own apartment. In an area of the world that's stuck in the mountains, I sometimes wonder what makes everyone so willing to settle for no heat, no water and never any hot water in exchange for slow internet access and great cell phone technology. In the end, this is the first time in Romania that I've thought the priorities here were a little off kilter. I love the internet, and staying connected via the web to everyone back home; but, and no offense to anyone in the US, I'd gladly change this for heat in my apartment this coming winter when it's expected to be about -40 degrees.

On a final note there appears to be no orphanges in my town (there's only 22,000 people here), but with the forrests around it appears that another possible project option is trail blazing and trail marking. Maybe when I come back a better name would be Grizzley Kenneth, or perhaps Kenneth of the North.
